# Afridata Brand Guidelines & Typography

## 1. Brand Overview
Afridata is a digital repository for African datasets, designed to collect, organize, and distribute high-quality data that represents Africa’s diverse economies, cultures, environments, and innovation landscapes.

The brand represents:
- Trustworthy data infrastructure for Africa
- Accessibility and openness in data sharing
- Modern, research-driven innovation
- Pan-African unity through information systems

The logo (Africa outline with connected nodes) symbolizes:
- Data connectivity across the continent
- Networked intelligence and collaboration
- Digital transformation of African knowledge systems

---

## 2. Brand Personality
Afridata should feel:

### Core Traits
- **Trustworthy** – reliable, academically credible
- **Modern** – clean, tech-forward, digital-native
- **Pan-African** – inclusive, diverse, continent-wide
- **Insightful** – data-driven, analytical, intelligent
- **Open** – transparent and accessible

### Tone of Voice
- Clear and professional
- Slightly academic but not overly technical
- Confident, not flashy
- Inclusive and empowering

Example:
> “Afridata provides structured, accessible datasets to support research, innovation, and policy development across Africa.”

---

## 3. Logo Usage Guidelines

### Primary Logo
- Use on white or very light backgrounds
- Maintain clear space equal to the height of the Africa icon around all sides

### Logo Don’ts
- Do not stretch or distort
- Do not change colors outside approved palette
- Do not place on busy backgrounds
- Do not add effects (shadows, gradients, outlines)

### Minimum Size
- Digital: 32px height minimum
- Print: 10mm height minimum

---

## 4. Color System

Afridata’s palette reflects trust, intelligence, and African identity.

### Primary Colors

- **Deep Ocean Blue**
  - Hex: #0B4F6C
  - Use: Primary brand identity, headers, navigation

- **Data Teal**
  - Hex: #1B998B
  - Use: Highlights, links, active states

### Secondary Colors

- **Soft Green (Growth/Data Integrity)**
  - Hex: #7BC67B

- **Slate Grey (Neutral UI)**
  - Hex: #4A5568

### Backgrounds

- **Off White**: #F7FAFC
- **Pure White**: #FFFFFF

### Accent (Use sparingly)
- Warm Amber: #F2C14E (for alerts, highlights, insights)

## 5. Typography System

Afridata’s typography should reflect clarity, structure, and modern digital professionalism.

### Primary Typeface: **Inter**
- Use for all UI elements and digital interfaces
- Excellent readability on screens
- Neutral and highly professional

Weights:
- Light (300): subtle UI elements
- Regular (400): body text
- Medium (500): emphasis
- SemiBold (600): headings
- Bold (700): titles and strong emphasis

---

### Secondary Typeface: **IBM Plex Serif**
- Use for editorial content, reports, and long-form research summaries
- Adds credibility and academic tone

Weights:
- Regular (400)
- Medium (500)
- SemiBold (600)

---

### Typography Hierarchy

#### H1 – Page Titles
- Font: Inter Bold
- Size: 40–48px
- Line height: 120%

#### H2 – Section Titles
- Font: Inter SemiBold
- Size: 28–32px

#### H3 – Subsections
- Font: Inter Medium
- Size: 20–24px

#### Body Text
- Font: Inter Regular
- Size: 14–16px
- Line height: 150–170%

#### Data Labels / UI Text
- Font: Inter Medium
- Size: 12–13px

#### Captions
- Font: Inter Light or Regular
- Size: 11–12px

---

## 6. Spacing & Layout System

Use an 8px grid system for consistency.

Spacing scale:
- 4px (xs)
- 8px (sm)
- 16px (md)
- 24px (lg)
- 32px (xl)
- 48px (2xl)

---

## 7. UI Design Principles

### Data-First Design
- Data visualization is primary, not decorative
- Charts and datasets should be readable and minimal

### Minimalism
- Avoid visual clutter
- Use whitespace generously

### Consistency
- Uniform components across all pages
- Standardized buttons, cards, and tables

### Accessibility
- Minimum contrast ratio: WCAG AA compliance
- Clear focus states for navigation

---

## 8. Iconography

- Style: Outline-based, minimal, geometric
- Stroke width: consistent (1.5px–2px)
- Rounded edges preferred for softness

Icons should represent:
- Data
- Connectivity
- Search
- Analytics
- Download/upload

---

## 9. Imagery Style

- Prefer abstract data visualizations
- African context imagery should be respectful, real, and documentary-style
- Avoid stereotypes or overly stock-like visuals

---

## 10. Component Styling

### Buttons
- Primary: Deep Ocean Blue background, white text
- Secondary: Transparent with teal border
- Radius: 8px

### Cards
- White background
- Subtle shadow (low elevation)
- Rounded corners (12px)

### Tables
- Minimal borders
- Alternating row shading (very light grey)

---

## 11. Data Visualization Style

- Use teal and blue gradients sparingly
- Prefer flat charts over 3D
- Ensure readability over decoration
- Highlight key insights rather than overwhelming detail

---

## 12. Brand Application Summary
Afridata should always feel like:

> A trusted African data infrastructure platform that is modern, open, and academically credible—built for researchers, developers, policymakers, and innovators.
